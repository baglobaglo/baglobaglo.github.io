Part C:

Issues with the current design?


The design gives no information to the guest what you can find in the different floors.
The buttons turns red when they are pressed. The color red could in some cases potentially give the guests
association with that something being wrong / not working.
When you press the open - and/or close door button, it does not show a color, unlike any of the other buttons
You dont neccesary expect these kind of button to give much feed back, but when they have panels to the right
of them, like the other buttons, you would expect them to give som visual feedback like the other buttons.
We got both an alarm and telephone button. What button does what? There is a small and short explanation
text above the buttons, but that only describes the telephone icon. When should you press what button?
Not necessarily intuitive.



Why is it good/bad ?


Gives visual and sound feedback. Overall clean and simple. Got the most essentials
It is not a big and complex hotel, but some visual information about the hotel/floor could be beneficial(?). Good with alignments, contrasts. We got signs for blind-people. Relativley low centered buttons for people in
wheelchair can press the buttons.
