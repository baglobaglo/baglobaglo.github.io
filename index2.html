Part B:



The most common elevator features i use must be buttons for the different floors. This is always
the case no matter how many floors there are or what type of elevator. Other than that,
I frequently use the close/open door button. This depends on how fast or slow the doors
open or closes. I have never used the alarm/telephone button in an elevator, luckily.
I rarely find myself using other features/buttons as well.
Based on my typical use, and what i would expect in an levator, the interface lets me use
these features easily.

With this interface it is quite straight forward. You would typically press the button
with the number of your floor, and then maybe press the close elevator door button. Thats it.
The elevator support the user by having a clean and simple interface. No random "cool" button
feature to confuse the guests. It also have a more or less standard interface, so people
who have used elevator before know what to do. (universal elevatur "rules").
The elevator provids feedback with both sounds and visuals. Depending on what features you use.
More about feedback was also mentioned in part C.
Luckily , there are not many mistakes you can make with a interface like this.
But as i wrote in part C, there are two button that seems to be an "alarm" button.
I think one of the buttons perhaps is to contact the hotel/reception(?) and the other is to
call the alarm sentral. But i dont know. It is not as intuitive or clear as i whould ecpect it to be.
If you are in an elevator and need help, you would likely contact the alarm sentral, and not the reception
i can imagne.
Also, having a star beside the number "1" on the button does not give any
reason for why that should be the reception/main floor.

Based on some of these feedbacks and thoughts about the desing. I have made a sketch
with some fun and different ideas. This is not necessarily the perfect solution, but its fun to
try something different than what you would normally find when you have a project like this.

Sketch idea:


Agian, not the perfect solution. This is to answer some of the things i have pointed out in C and B. I was thinking of an interface
where the guests can get an idea/visual representation of what they can find and except from the elevator. There is also a plan that an elevator will
follow you as you move up and down in the hotel. From the sketch i also tried to show that the buttons should have some sound and visual feedback.
I put all the buttons I found necessary in a alignment to the left, and the hotel sketch to the right. I removed the two alarm/telephone buttons and made just one alarm button.
I think this is simpler and cleaner.
